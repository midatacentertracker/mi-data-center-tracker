Standards & sourcing

How this tracker works

Michigan Data Center Tracker is an independent index of public information. It is not an official database, a substitute for public records, or an endorsement of any source, project or political position.

01

Original wording

Tracker headlines and summaries are written in our own words. We link readers to the originating report, filing, notice or post for the full context and do not reproduce another publisher’s headline as our own.

02

Source hierarchy

Official agendas, minutes, filings, ordinances and direct company statements come first. Credible local and statewide reporting may establish a “reported” record when a primary document is not yet available.

03

Confidence labels

Confirmed means supported by an official or direct source. Reported means supported by a named news source. A public signal may be listed only when its original post is directly linked and clearly contextualized.

04

Map precision

Most markers identify a municipality, not a parcel. Approximate coordinates are disclosed. The linked source is always the authority for site location and status.

05

Status metrics

At-a-glance figures are snapshots from the cited source and date. Different sources may count proposals, operating facilities, utility inquiries or moratoria differently, so labels describe what each number includes.

06

Public-source monitor

X and Reddit material documents the public conversation; inclusion is not endorsement. Automated feeds may surface candidates, but publication should retain direct links, source identity, context and editorial review.

Corrections

Accuracy beats speed.

Send corrections, missing documents or stronger primary sources to michigandatacentertracker@gmail.com. Material corrections should be updated in the tracker data and reflected in the editorial update timestamp.
